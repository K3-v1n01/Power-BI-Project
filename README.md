# Power-BI-Project
Samsung Supply Chain And Logistics
The main objective of this project is to analyze Samsung’s supply chain 
performance by examining:

• Supplier performance 
• Inventory management 
• Manufacturing efficiency 
• Shipment and delivery performance 
• Sales and revenue insights 
• Customer & channel performance 

The goal is to identify inefficiencies, optimize logistics, reduce delays, 
improve inventory turnover, and maximize profitability. 

The overall business performance is financially strong, with high revenue and a healthy profit margin of around 27%, showing that the company has strong 
demand. Online and retailer channels contribute almost equally to revenue and smartphones are the major profit drivers indicating a well-performing 
product portfolio. 

However, operational efficiency needs improvement. The perfect order rate is 75% meaning delivery delays and shipment issues still affect performance. 
Some carriers contribute more to delays and logistics bottlenecks reduce service reliability. Inventory levels are also high with products staying in 
storage for long periods which increases holding costs and affects cash flow. Additionally, defective units highlight the need for better quality control. 

Overall, the company is commercially successful, but improving logistics efficiency, inventory management, and supplier coordination can further 
increase profitability and customer satisfaction.
